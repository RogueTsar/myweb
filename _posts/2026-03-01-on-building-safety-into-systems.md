There is a version of the alignment problem that is purely technical: how do you train a model that does what you mean? And there is a version that is institutional: who decides what "what you mean" means, and how do those decisions get made at scale?

I've spent the last year working across both sides. At Oxford, the work was technical -- interpretability methods, evaluation pipelines, understanding how models represent and pursue goals. At KPMG, it was governance -- risk matrices, compliance frameworks, the slow machinery of institutional adoption.

The gap between these two worlds is where the most interesting problems live. A technically aligned system deployed by an institution that doesn't understand it is still dangerous. A perfectly governed system with no technical teeth is still theatre.

I think the next generation of AI safety work needs people who can hold both frames simultaneously. Not because they're better, but because the problems demand it.
