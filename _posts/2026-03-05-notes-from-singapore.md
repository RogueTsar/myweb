Singapore's approach to AI governance is distinctive. Where the EU leans toward prescriptive regulation and the US toward industry self-governance, Singapore occupies a pragmatic middle ground -- frameworks like the Model AI Governance Framework and IMDA's guidelines prefer principles over hard mandates.

Having worked here across multiple roles -- from KPMG's advisory practice to SMU's research labs to policy conversations with IMDA -- I've seen how this approach plays out in practice. Companies take the frameworks seriously because Singapore is small enough that regulatory relationships are personal. There's no hiding behind scale.

The AI safety ecosystem in Asia-Pacific is still early. AI Safety Asia is one of the few organisations building infrastructure for this community. When I started volunteering there, I was struck by how much of the work is simply making connections visible -- between researchers in different countries, between technical and governance communities, between what's happening in the labs and what's happening in the ministries.

Building these bridges is slow work. But it's the kind of work that compounds.
